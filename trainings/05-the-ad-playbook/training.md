
**The fundamental shift:** You went from being the targeting expert to being the creative expert. The AI is now better than any human at finding the right audience. Your job is to give it the right MESSAGE.

**You running separate retarget campaigns is like backseat-driving an AI that's better than you at the wheel.** Get out of its way. Feed it creative. Let it work.

## SECTION 3: THE CREATIVE FRAMEWORK — Static vs. Video vs. Carousel (7:30 - 11:00)

### Format Diversity Matrix

Andromeda rewards diversity. Here's your creative mix:

| Format | % of Mix | Strengths | Best For |
|--------|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Short Video (15-30s)** | 35% | Highest engagement, hook-driven | Education, myth-busting, quick tips |
| **Static Image** | 25% | Quick to produce, bold messaging | Stats, testimonials, offers, before/after |
| **Long Video (60-90s)** | 15% | Story-driven, deeper trust | Patient journeys, doctor authority, education |
| **Carousel (3-5 slides)** | 15% | Interactive, step-by-step | Process explanation, multiple benefits, comparison |
| **UGC-Style** | 10% | Authenticity, relatability | Patient reviews, candid moments, real results |

### Video Ad Rules (Post-Andromeda)

**The Hook (First 3 Seconds) — This is EVERYTHING**

Andromeda scores your first 3 seconds separately. If people scroll past, the AI stops showing your ad. Period.

**Hook formulas that work:**

| Hook Type | Example | Why It Works |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **Bold Claim** | "90% of back pain has nothing to do with your back" | Challenges beliefs |
| **Question** | "Still waking up stiff every morning?" | Speaks to their pain |
| **Statistic** | "73% of people with back pain are making it worse" | Authority + fear |
| **Pattern Interrupt** | [Doctor cracks own neck on camera] | Shocking visual |
| **Curiosity Gap** | "The ONE thing your chiropractor isn't telling you" | Creates need to watch |
| **Result Lead** | "This patient couldn't tie her shoes 6 weeks ago..." | Proof first |

**Video structure:**
```
0-3s:   HOOK — Stop the scroll
3-10s:  PROBLEM — Agitate the pain
10-20s: SOLUTION — Your mechanism/approach
20-25s: PROOF — Results, testimonials, credentials
25-30s: CTA — One clear next step
```

### Static Ad Rules

**Design principles for healthcare ads:**
- **One message per image** — don't try to say everything
- **Large, readable text** — most people view on mobile
- **Faces convert better** — doctor photos, patient photos
- **Before/after** — the most powerful static format for clinics
- **Contrast** — dark text on light, or light text on dark. No busy backgrounds.

**Static ad types that perform:**

| Type | Example Copy | Visual |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **Pain Point** | "Tired of waking up in pain every morning?" | Person holding their back in bed |
| **Stat Bomb** | "87% of our patients report relief after visit 1" | Bold number, clean design |
| **Testimonial** | "I can finally play with my kids again — Sarah M." | Photo of Sarah + star rating |
| **Offer Stack** | "Free Assessment + Treatment Plan ($350 value)" | Value list with checkmarks |
| **Comparison** | "Chiro vs. PT vs. Meds — Which Actually Works?" | 3-column comparison |

### Carousel Best Practices

- **Slide 1:** Hook — the reason to keep swiping
- **Slides 2-4:** Value/education — one point per slide
- **Final Slide:** CTA — clear offer + link
- **Design:** Consistent branding across slides, numbered, feels like a mini-guide

## SECTION 4: THE HORMOZI TESTING SYSTEM — 6 Angles × 5 Hooks (11:00 - 14:00)

This is the framework that separates amateurs from professionals.

### Step 1: Identify Your 6 Angles

An "angle" is the core REASON someone would want your service. Different people care about different things.

**For a chiropractic clinic:**

| Angle | Core Message | Target Emotion |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **Pain Relief** | "End your chronic back pain" | Desperation |
| **Performance** | "Move better, perform better" | Ambition |
| **Prevention** | "Fix it now before surgery is the only option" | Fear |
| **Lifestyle** | "Play with your kids, sleep through the night" | Desire |
| **Authority** | "30 years experience, 10,000+ patients treated" | Trust |
| **Social Proof** | "See why 500+ patients gave us 5 stars" | Validation |

### Step 2: Create 5 Hooks for Each Angle

For the **Pain Relief** angle:
1. "Still popping Advil every morning? There's a better way."
2. "The real reason your back pain keeps coming back"
3. "I've treated 10,000 patients. This is the #1 mistake I see."
4. "Watch: This patient went from daily pain to pain-free in 3 weeks"
5. "Stop stretching — it's making your back pain WORSE"

For the **Fear/Prevention** angle:
1. "3 signs your body is breaking down — and you don't know it"
2. "Ignore this and you'll be in surgery within 5 years"
3. "The difference between a $200 fix now and a $50,000 surgery later"
4. "Your MRI looks fine? Here's why you're still in pain."
5. "If you can't touch your toes, your back is a ticking time bomb"

**6 angles × 5 hooks = 30 unique ads.** That's your testing arsenal.

### Step 3: The Weekly Testing Cycle

**Week 1: Launch**
- Load all 30 creatives (mix of formats)
- Set budget to $50-100/day
- Let Andromeda distribute spend for 5-7 days
- DO NOT TOUCH THE ADS — let the AI learn

**Week 2: Analyze**
- Which 3-5 creatives got the most spend? (These are winners)
- Which creatives have CTR >1.5%? (Strong hooks)
- Which creatives have CPL <$40? (Efficient converters)
- Kill the bottom 50% of performers

**Week 3: Iterate**
- **70% of budget** → Variations of the top 2-3 winners (new hooks, same angle)
- **20% of budget** → Winners remixed in new formats (top static → make it a video)
- **10% of budget** → Completely new angles (always testing fresh concepts)

**Week 4+: Repeat**
- Refresh 2-3 creatives per week
- Kill fatigued creatives (when CPL rises 30%+ from baseline)
- Keep the testing engine running ALWAYS

### The Creative Fatigue Problem

Post-Andromeda, ad fatigue happens FASTER because:
- The AI quickly identifies winners and scales them hard
- Your best ad gets shown more → burns out faster
- If you don't have fresh creative ready, performance crashes

**Rule of thumb:** No creative should run more than 3-4 weeks without a refresh. Top performers might last 2 weeks before fatigue sets in.

**The solution:** Build a creative production pipeline. You need to be producing 5-10 new ad concepts per week. This doesn't mean professional video production — it means your phone, your face, and a hook.

## SECTION 5: THE AD FUNNEL — Where Your Ad Sends People (14:00 - 17:00)

Your ad is only half the equation. The other half is what happens AFTER the click.

### Funnel Option 1: Lead Form (Meta Native)

```
AD → Meta Lead Form → Thank You Screen → Automated SMS/Email → Call
```

**Pros:** Low friction, stays in-app, higher volume of leads
**Cons:** Lower quality leads (easy to fill out = tire-kickers)
**Best for:** Godfather offers, free assessments, high-volume clinics

**Lead Form Best Practices:**
- ☐ Pre-fill enabled (name, email auto-populate)
- ☐ Maximum 3 custom questions (less = more completions)
- ☐ Custom question 1: "What's your main concern?" (dropdown)
- ☐ Custom question 2: "When would you like to come in?" (this week / next week / just exploring)
- ☐ Thank you screen: "We'll call you within 5 minutes!" + phone number
- ☐ CRM integration: leads auto-push to your system for instant follow-up

### Funnel Option 2: Landing Page

```
AD → Landing Page → Form Submission → Automated Follow-up → Call
```

**Pros:** Higher quality leads, more education before conversion, better tracking
**Cons:** Extra step = lower volume, page needs to be fast + mobile-optimized
**Best for:** Grand Slam Offers, higher-ticket programs, education-heavy funnels

**Landing Page Formula:**

```
SECTION 1: Headline + Subhead (match the ad hook exactly)
SECTION 2: Pain agitation (3-4 bullet points of their problems)
SECTION 3: Your solution (mechanism — HOW you fix it differently)
SECTION 4: Value Stack (everything included — with dollar values)
SECTION 5: Social Proof (3-5 testimonials with photos)
SECTION 6: Risk Reversal (your guarantee)
SECTION 7: CTA (form — name, phone, email, "What's your main concern?")
SECTION 8: FAQ (handle 4-5 top objections)
```

**Critical rules:**
- Page must load in <3 seconds on mobile
- CTA above the fold (visible without scrolling)
- Phone number clickable
- No navigation menu (don't let them leave)
- Match the ad's visual style (no disconnect between ad and page)

### Funnel Option 3: Quiz Funnel (Highest Quality)

```
AD → Quiz ("Which Treatment Is Right For You?") → Results + Offer → Book
```

**Pros:** Highest engagement, self-qualification, feels personalized
**Cons:** More complex to build, takes longer to complete
**Best for:** Multi-service clinics, health optimization, weight loss

**Quiz structure:**
1. "What's your primary concern?" (back pain / neck pain / headaches / posture)
2. "How long have you been dealing with this?" (weeks / months / years)
3. "What have you tried?" (nothing / stretching / other providers / meds)
4. "What's your goal?" (pain-free / active lifestyle / avoid surgery / sleep better)
5. RESULTS: "Based on your answers, our [Specific Program] is perfect for you. Here's your custom plan..."

### The Funnel Speed Rule

No matter which funnel you use, the #1 rule from Training 1 still applies:

**CALL EVERY LEAD WITHIN 5 MINUTES.**

The best funnel in the world is worthless if leads sit in your inbox for 2 hours. Set up:
- ☐ Instant SMS: "Hey [Name]! We got your info. Someone from our team will call you in the next few minutes."
- ☐ Instant CRM notification to your intake coordinator
- ☐ If no answer in 5 min → automatic voicemail drop + follow-up text
- ☐ Follow-up sequence: Call Day 1, 2, 3, 5, 7 until you reach them

### 🩺 Clinical Sidebar: EVIDENCE-BASED MARKETING

You don't prescribe based on vibes. You follow the hierarchy: RCTs > cohort studies > case reports > expert opinion.

**Your marketing should follow the same evidence hierarchy:**

| Evidence Level | Clinical | Marketing Equivalent |
|---------------|----------|---------------------|
| **RCT** | Double-blind, randomized | A/B test with statistical significance |
| **Cohort Study** | Prospective observational | Tracking a campaign over 90 days |
| **Case Report** | N=1 anecdote | "My friend's clinic did this and it worked" |
| **Expert Opinion** | Eminence, not evidence | "My marketing guy says we should..." |

*"You'd never prescribe a drug based on one case report. So why are you spending $5,000/month on an ad strategy based on 'my buddy said it worked for him'? Run the A/B test. Follow the evidence."*

You use **Relative Risk** and **Odds Ratio** to compare interventions. Let's look at the marketing data through that lens:

| Comparison | RR / OR | Clinical Translation |
|-----------|---------|---------------------|
| Speed to lead <5 min vs >30 min | **RR = 21x** | 21x more likely to qualify |
| Education-based vs pitch-based marketing | **OR = 3.91** | 391% higher conversion |
| Full-funnel vs single-stage | **RR = 1.45** | 45% higher ROI |
| Respond in <1 min vs average | **OR = 3.91** | 391% more conversions |

*"If I showed you a treatment with an NNT of 5 and a relative risk reduction of 21x, you'd prescribe it to every patient. Speed-to-lead has those numbers. Why aren't you prescribing it to your business?"*

## SECTION 6: THE METRICS DASHBOARD — What to Track (17:00 - 19:00)

You can't improve what you don't measure. Here's your ad dashboard:

### Primary Metrics (Check Daily)

| Metric | Target | What It Tells You |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **Cost Per Lead (CPL)** | <$30-50 | Is your creative + funnel efficient? |
| **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** | >1.5% | Is your hook stopping the scroll? |
| **Cost Per Click (CPC)** | <$2-5 | Is the platform rewarding your content? |
| **Lead Volume** | Depends on budget | Are you generating enough pipeline? |

### Secondary Metrics (Check Weekly)

| Metric | Target | What It Tells You |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **Hook Rate (video)** | >30% watch 3s+ | Is your opening strong enough? |
| **Landing Page Conversion** | >15% | Is your page compelling? |
| **Lead-to-Appointment Rate** | >25% | Is your follow-up fast & effective? |
| **Appointment Show Rate** | >80% | Are your reminders working? |
| **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)** | <$150-250 | Total cost per new patient |
| **Frequency** | <3 per 7 days | Are you over-saturating the same people? |

### Revenue Metrics (Check Monthly)

| Metric | Target | What It Tells You |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)** | >5x | For every $1 spent, you get $5+ back |
| **Patient Lifetime Value (LTV)** | $2,000-20,000 | How much is each patient worth long-term? |
| **LTV:CPA Ratio** | >10:1 | Is your acquisition cost sustainable? |
| **Payback Period** | <30 days | How fast do you make your ad money back? |

### The Diagnostic Framework

When something's not working, diagnose systematically:

```
Low CTR (<1%) → HOOK problem → Test new hooks/angles
    ↓
High CTR, Low Page Conversion (<10%) → PAGE problem → Fix landing page
    ↓
Good Page Conversion, Low Appointments (<15%) → FOLLOW-UP problem → Speed to lead
    ↓
Good Appointments, Low Close Rate (<40%) → OFFER problem → Fix your Grand Slam Offer
    ↓
Everything works but no scale → BUDGET problem → Increase spend gradually (20%/week)
```

**Follow the chain.** Don't throw money at ads when the problem is your landing page. Don't redesign your page when the problem is your follow-up speed. Diagnose first, then fix the weakest link.

## SECTION 7: GOOGLE ADS — Capturing High-Intent Searchers (19:00 - 21:00)

While Meta ads build awareness, **Google Ads capture demand that already exists.**

Someone typing "chiropractor near me" at 10pm on a Tuesday is READY. They're in pain right now. They want help right now. Your job is to be the first thing they see.

### Google Ads Structure for Clinics

**Campaign 1: Brand Search**
- Keywords: Your clinic name, doctor name
- Why: Prevent competitors from stealing people searching for YOU
- Budget: $200-500/month (low cost, high conversion)

**Campaign 2: Service Search (High Intent)**
- Keywords: "chiropractor near me", "back pain doctor [city]", "physical therapy [city]"
- Why: People ready to book — highest conversion rate
- Budget: 50% of your Google budget
- Expected CPL: $30-60

**Campaign 3: Symptom/Problem Search**
- Keywords: "why does my back hurt", "sciatica treatment", "herniated disc help"
- Why: Problem-aware people researching solutions (MOFU)
- Budget: 30% of your Google budget
- Expected CPL: $40-80 (lower intent, but larger volume)

**Campaign 4: Local Service Ads (LSA)**
- Shows your business card at the TOP of Google with reviews + "Google Guaranteed" badge
- Pay per lead (not per click) — typically $25-50/lead
- Requires background check and Google verification
- **This is the highest-trust ad format on Google — prioritize this**

### Google Ad Copy Formula

**Headline 1:** [Service] in [City] — [Benefit]
**Headline 2:** [Social Proof] — [Differentiator]
**Headline 3:** [Offer/CTA]
**Description:** [Pain point] → [Solution] → [Proof] → [CTA]

**Example:**
> **Back Pain Relief in Austin — Feel Better in 1 Visit**
> **500+ 5-Star Reviews — Board Certified Specialists**
> **Free Assessment — Book Online Today**
>
> Tired of waking up in pain? Our proven treatment protocol has helped 10,000+ patients get back to living. Free initial assessment ($250 value). Book in 60 seconds — no referral needed.

## SECTION 8: TESTING FUNNELS — How to Know What's Working (21:00 - 23:00)

### The Testing Hierarchy

Test in this order (most impact → least impact):

```
1. OFFER (what you're selling) — 10x impact
2. HOOK (first 3 seconds / headline) — 5x impact
3. CREATIVE FORMAT (video vs static vs carousel) — 3x impact
4. BODY COPY (main message) — 2x impact
5. AUDIENCE (with Andromeda, this barely matters anymore) — 1x impact
```

**Most people test audiences.** That's the LEAST impactful thing to test post-Andromeda. Test your OFFER first, your HOOK second.

### How to Run a Proper Test

**Rule 1: Change ONE variable at a time.**
If you change the hook AND the image AND the offer simultaneously, you won't know what worked.

**Rule 2: Give it enough budget.**
Don't judge a creative on $10 of spend. Minimum $50-100 per creative before making decisions.

**Rule 3: Give it enough time.**
Andromeda needs 3-7 days to learn. Don't kill an ad after 24 hours.

**Rule 4: Use the right metric for the right test.**
- Testing hooks? Look at CTR and Hook Rate
- Testing offers? Look at CPL and Conversion Rate
- Testing funnels? Look at CPA and ROAS

### A/B Testing Calendar

| Week | Test | What You're Learning |
|------|------|---------------------|
| 1 | 6 angles × 1 format (static) | Which ANGLES resonate? |
| 2 | Top 2 angles × 5 hooks each | Which HOOKS stop the scroll? |
| 3 | Top 3 hook+angle combos × 3 formats | Which FORMAT performs best? |
| 4 | Winner variations + 2 new angles | Can we scale the winner? Is there a new winner? |
| 5+ | Ongoing: 70% winners / 20% iterations / 10% new | Continuous optimization |

### The Kill Criteria

When to turn off an ad:

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| CPL 2x above target after $100 spend | Kill it |
| CTR <0.8% after 1,000 impressions | Kill it |
| Frequency >3 in 7 days | Refresh creative |
| CPL rising 30%+ from baseline over 7 days | Creative fatigue — pause and iterate |
| CPA above LTV | Kill the campaign and diagnose |

## SECTION 9: THE COMPLETE AD WORKFLOW (23:00 - 25:00)

Here's your weekly rhythm for running ads like a pro:

### Monday: Review & Plan
- Check weekend performance
- Identify top 3 and bottom 3 creatives
- Plan this week's new creatives (2-3 concepts)

### Tuesday-Wednesday: Create
- Film 2-3 new video concepts (phone is fine)
- Design 2-3 new static concepts
- Write new hooks and copy variations

### Thursday: Launch & Kill
- Upload new creatives to the campaign
- Kill underperformers from last week
- Adjust budgets (scale winners by 20% max)

### Friday: Optimize
- Check lead quality (talk to your intake team)
- Review funnel metrics (page conversion, lead form completion)
- Verify speed-to-lead (are leads getting called in <5 min?)

### The Production Calendar

You don't need a video team. You need a PHONE and a SYSTEM:

| Day | Content | Time Required |
|-----|---------|--------------|
| Monday | 2 quick tip videos (doctor on camera) | 15 min |
| Tuesday | 3 static designs (Canva or similar) | 30 min |
